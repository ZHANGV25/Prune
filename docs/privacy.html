Privacy Policy

Effective April 20, 2026

Prune is a photo-cleanup app that runs entirely on your device. This policy explains what data the app accesses, what leaves your device, and who we share it with.

In one sentence: Your photos never leave your device. We collect only what's needed to verify subscriptions and diagnose crashes.

1. What the app accesses on your device

Photo library

Prune reads photos and videos from your Apple Photos library to show them to you for review. When you confirm a deletion, Prune asks the iOS Photos framework to move those items to Recently Deleted, where Apple keeps them for 30 days before permanent removal. This is the same behavior as deleting from the Apple Photos app.

Your photos and videos never leave your device. Prune does not upload them to any server, and does not analyze them using cloud AI.

App storage

Prune stores a small amount of data on your device using Apple's UserDefaults:

  • Local identifiers of photos you have already reviewed, so we don't show them to you again.
  • A daily counter of swipes used (for the free-tier limit of 50/day).
  • Whether you have completed onboarding, and whether you have an active Pro subscription.

None of this information is uploaded to us or to third parties.

2. What leaves your device, and why

Subscriptions (RevenueCat)

We use RevenueCat to manage in-app subscriptions. When you start, cancel, or restore a purchase, a pseudonymous RevenueCat user ID and purchase-history records are shared with RevenueCat so your Pro status can be verified across sessions and devices.

RevenueCat does not track you across apps or websites. No advertising identifiers are shared.

Apple (App Store)

In-app purchases are processed by Apple. Your payment information is handled by Apple under Apple's privacy policy; Prune and Isotropic Studios never see your payment details.

Crash reports and performance data

If the app crashes, Apple may send a diagnostic report to us through its standard developer tooling. These reports do not include your photos, and you control this in Settings > Privacy & Security > Analytics & Improvements > Share with App Developers.

3. What we do not do

  • We do not show ads. We do not integrate advertising SDKs.
  • We do not track you across other apps or websites.
  • We do not collect your Apple advertising identifier (IDFA). Prune does not call the App Tracking Transparency framework.
  • We do not upload your photos, videos, or any media content from your library.
  • We do not sell your personal information to anyone, under any definition of "sell" used by US state privacy laws.

4. Children's privacy

Prune is rated 4+ and is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 13.

5. Your rights

Because Prune stores nearly everything locally, the easiest way to "delete your data" is to delete the app from your device. To delete your RevenueCat subscription record, email us at vhzhang2020@gmail.com with the bundle ID com.isotropicstudios.Prune and the approximate date of purchase.

If you are in California, the EU, the UK, or another jurisdiction with data-protection laws, you have rights to access, correct, port, and delete data we hold about you. Because the only such data is a pseudonymous RevenueCat record tied to your purchase, the easiest path is the email above.

6. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes, we'll update the "Effective" date above and, if you have the app installed, show a notice the next time you open it.
