Frequently asked

Where do deleted photos go?

To your Recently Deleted album in the Apple Photos app. Apple holds them there for 30 days, then permanently removes them. You can restore any photo during that window by opening Photos > Albums > Recently Deleted > Recover.

Does Prune upload my photos?

No. Photos never leave your device. Prune uses Apple's Photos framework to read and delete; there is no cloud processing. See our Privacy Policy.

How do I cancel my subscription?

On iOS: Settings > your name > Subscriptions > Pruned Pro > Cancel Subscription. Cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing period; you keep access until then.

How do I get a refund?

Refunds are handled by Apple, not by us. Request one at reportaproblem.apple.com — sign in with your Apple ID, find the Prune purchase, and choose "Request a refund." Apple decides on each request.

Free tier has a 50-swipe daily limit. When does it reset?

At midnight in your device's local timezone.

Why am I not seeing some of my photos?

The free "All Photos" feed skips photos you've already swiped on. You can reset this by deleting and reinstalling Prune. Pro users also get feeds filtered by date range, selfies, screenshots, videos, and favorites.

I accidentally deleted something important.

Open the Apple Photos app → Albums → Recently Deleted → tap the photo → Recover. Apple keeps deleted items for 30 days.

Does Prune work on iPad?

Yes. The interface is optimized for iPhone but runs on iPad too.

What iOS version do I need?

iOS 17 or later.
